BOWL Historical vs BOWL Cap — team season records and identity

SHARED BASELINE (naming + logos)
- Both leagues use the same rules: per-league team_identity_history.csv, shared
  bowl_historical logo assets, and the app’s era logo/name resolution (Cap also
  scans bowl_historical for timeline files).
- Keep franchise display names and logo eras aligned in team_identity_history.csv
  for both raw folders unless a league intentionally diverges on branding.

TEAM_SEASON_RECORDS_TEMPLATE.CSV — DATA SCOPE
- Through season 1941-42 (inclusive): Historical and Cap should stay the same
  NHL-history baseline (stats, results, standings narrative) where both files
  track the same era.
- From 1942-43 onward: each league owns its own alternate history. Membership
  (which FHM team ids exist) can match, but outcomes — W/L, playoffs, awards,
  leaderboards driven from these CSVs — must be edited independently. Do not
  bulk-copy Cap rows into Historical (or vice versa) when updating post-1941
  seasons.

FRANCHISE IDS
- Cap’s Minnesota North Stars → Dallas lineage uses FHM team id 12 in
  bowl_cap/team_data.csv. Historical’s expansion Minnesota club may use a
  different id (e.g. 119) in bowl_historical/team_data.csv; identity rows are
  keyed by that league’s team_fhm_id.
